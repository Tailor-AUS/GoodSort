## Tailor Vision ↔ GoodSort commercial agreement

**Pricing:** GoodSort pays Tailor **1.85× Tailor's COGS** per classification call, invoiced monthly in arrears. Tailor provides COGS breakdown quarterly for verification.

**Term:** 12 months, auto-renewing, 60-day exit notice.

**Key clauses:**
- Tailor owns the vision model and classification output schema
- GoodSort owns its scan records and user data
- **Tailor is responsible for keeping CDS eligibility data current** (no downstream overrides by GoodSort required)
- Australian data residency
- Liability cap at 12 months of fees
- Standard SaaS SLAs (uptime target, latency target)

Tailor does NOT hold equity in GoodSort under this arrangement. The commercial relationship is arms-length, based solely on per-call fees. (If Tailor wants equity, that is a separate negotiation on separate terms.)

## Governance

- **Transfer restrictions:** Standard — founder shares cannot be transferred without board approval for the first 4 years.
- **Drag-along / tag-along:** Standard majority trigger at 50%+1 of ordinary shares.
- **Pre-emptive rights:** All founders have pro-rata pre-emption on new issues.
- **Reserved matters (requiring board + 75% shareholder approval):**
  - Changes to share capital / new share classes
  - Sale of the company or material assets
  - Taking on debt above $100k
  - Material changes to the Tailor Vision agreement
  - Dismissal of CEO
- **Deadlock:** If the two directors disagree on a reserved matter, escalate to shareholder vote.

## Option pool (future)

Reserve for post-incorporation: 10–15% ESOP created pre-first-raise for future hires. Funded from all founders pro rata at creation.

## Confidentiality & non-compete

All founders agree to standard confidentiality and to refrain from competing with GoodSort in the CDS / container recycling / consumer sorting space for the duration of their involvement + 12 months post-departure.

**Exception:** Tailor Vision API is explicitly not a competing business — it is a supplier.
